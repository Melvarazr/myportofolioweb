Pahamify

Online Learning Platform Enhancement

Figma Design Project Document 1 Project Document 2

Project Description

Pahamify is one of Indonesia's most popular online learning platforms for high school students, featuring engaging video lessons, quizzes, and try-outs. This project focused on improving user experience in the Bank Soal (Question Bank) and try-out features, addressing common pain points such as confusing navigation, lack of real-time feedback, and limited interactivity. The redesign leverages Design Thinking methodology, prioritizing intuitive workflows and visual clarity to support student success and engagement.

Key Improvements

Simplified Login & Registration Flow

Previously, users found it confusing to access the login and registration pages. The new design places clear login/register buttons directly on the homepage for easier access.

Clearer Subject Categorization

Users were confused by the difference between "Matematika" and "Matematika IPA." These categories were renamed to "Matematika Wajib" and "Matematika Minat," making subject selection more intuitive.

Added Real-Time Timer

Originally, students didn't know how much time they spent on questions. The new version includes a visible timer, helping users manage their time during practice.

Introduced Question Flagging

Previously, there was no way to mark uncertain questions. Now, students can flag questions they're unsure about and easily return to them before submitting.

Improved Review & Feedback Experience

Before, reviewing answers was confusing and feedback was only available after searching. Now, results and correct/incorrect answers are shown immediately after finishing, with an easier-to-understand review page.

Richer Explanations

Explanations for answers were previously text-only, making it harder for some users to understand. The updated design includes both text and video explanations for better learning.

Stronger Visual Hierarchy and Accessibility

Fonts and color contrasts have been improved. Answer choices are now bolded and flagged questions use more noticeable colors, making it easier for students to read and navigate the content.

5
